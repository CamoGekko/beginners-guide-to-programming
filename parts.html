Parts of a Computer

Central Processing Unit

The Central Processing Unit or CPU is one of the most important parts of a PC. It mostly does math for your everyday needs, CPUs are just a block of silicon that has been engraved by a precision laser to route power to where its needed in the chip. Various functions are lasered onto the CPU like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Because a CPU is physically programmed, a user cannot edit the underlying "programming" of the chip without a precision engraver, if you are reading this you probably don't have a presision engraver.
